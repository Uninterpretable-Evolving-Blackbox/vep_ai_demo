## User Query
VEP says my GWAS hit is a regulatory_region_variant overlapping an enhancer. What should I do next?

## Explanation
The VEP annotation indicates that your gene variant overlaps with an enhancer, specifically as a `regulatory_region_variant`. Overlapping regulatory elements like this can affect how strongly genes are expressed, even if the impact on the encoded protein sequence is subtle.

### Why VEP Considered It Important
VEP annotated your result as a `regulatory_region_variant` because it found the variant within 5 kilobases (kb) of an enhancer region. Enhancers are sequences that increase or decrease how strongly genes are transcribed into mRNA, without altering what proteins those mRNAs code for.

### What to Check Next
1. **Enhancer Activity**: Use a tool like ChromHMM or DNase I Footprint Assay data to understand if your enhancer is highly active and likely has functional relevance. These tools can tell you which genes are activated by the enhancer when it's working normally.
   
2. **Transcription Factor Binding**: Enhancers often bind transcription factors (TFs). Check the specific proteins that normally bind this region in your gene of interest. If these TFs are known to upregulate the expression of your gene, having an enhancer may be significant.

3. **Expression Quantitative Trait Loci (eQTL) Data**: See if there's evidence for direct association between genetic variants and expression levels using eQTL data from genomics tools like GTEx or Roadmap Epigenomics. This can confirm whether this regulatory variant affects mRNA-level activity, even in the absence of mutations that impact protein function.

4. **Transcript Quantification Data**: Verify if your target gene is expressed at significantly different levels depending on how close it is to the enhancer region. You might also investigate the effects on other genes nearby as well.

### Summary
Given this annotation, understanding whether this regulatory variant interacts with known TFs and influences gene expression will be crucial. Explore these data sources for enhanced insight into how your variant alters transcriptional control.
